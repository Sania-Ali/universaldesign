Cheap All-Terrain Wheelchair

MIT engineer Amos Winter is working to design a wheelchair that costs less than $200, is usable indoors, allows travel of 5 km/day on varied terrain, and is locally repairable. The inspiration of the project is to serve wheelchair users in underdeveloped countries. The design uses bicycle parts and a hand-lever system. The design process involves working directly with local mechanics and wheelchair users. Click, "Amos Winter" to learn more about the project.

Dancer: Alice Sheppard

"Waiting for Earthquakes"

Dancer Moon Ribas created this piece titled, "Waiting for Earthquakes." Ribas wears sensors that allow her to feel earthquakes anywhere around the world. If there are no earthquakes, there is no dance. Watch the full performance by clicking, "Moon Ribas" in the text.

Leading the Bionic Age

Hugh Herr is the head of the Biomechatronics group at MIT Media Lab. His team developed the first autonomous exoskeleton to reduce the metabolic cost of human walking and the world's first bionic lower leg, called BiOM Ankle System. Learn more about the technology and other projects, by clicking "Hugh Herr" in the text.

Cheetah Legs

Van Phillips developed flexible spinter-legs, nicknamed "Cheetah Legs" for athlete Aimee Mullins in 1995. Today, 90% of Paralympic atheletes use these bending blade legs. Read more about Phillips and his invention by clicking, "Van Phillips" in the text.

Move2Learn

Move2Learn is an international research-practitioner project with the goal to understand the role of embodied interaction in young children's learning about science. Movement influences thought and thoughts influence movement. Research is currently underway in six locations around the US and UK. Click, "research-practioner project" to learn more about the work.
