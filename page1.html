Sound Art

Christine Sun Kim is a sound artist who explores how sound operates in society. She uses drawing, performance, and video forms to convey different relationships with sound. Her latest work, titled "The Sound of Obsessing," is featured in the MIT Media Lab and is pictured next to the text. To learn more about her work click, "Christine Sun Kim"

AI Cognitive Assistance

Chieko Asakawa is an IBM Fellow working to create technology that assists everyday life through the power of artificial intelligence (AI). Her lab has created an indoor navigation app to help individuals with visual impairments walk around independently. Asakawa is also working on an AI suitcase to help individuals travel indepedently. Learn more about her work by clicking, "Chieko Asakawa" 

Vibratory Vest

Dr. Eagleman's lab is leveraging the power of sensory substition, "a non-invasive technique for circumventing the loss of one sense by feeding its information through another channel." The vest allows individuals with deafness or other hearing impairments to perceive auditory information through small vibrations on their torso. Learn more about sensory substitution and the project by clicking, "Dr. Eagleman's lab."

SensorNets

MIT researchers have developed a bioinspired electronic skin that includes a network of sensors. The sensors can detect proxemic, tactile, and environmental changes. Read more about the project by clicking, "bioinspired electronic skin" in the text.

Hearing Color

Artist and cyborg rights activist, Neil Harbisson uses an "eyeborg" to hear the sounds of visible, infrared, and ultraviolet light. Neil was born with Achromatopsia (total color blindess) and uses an antenna to percieve electromagnetic radiation that is transmitted as audible vibrations in his skull. He is known as, "the world's first cyborg," and advocates for cyborg rights. To learn more about Neil and his sensory abilities, click "eyeborg." in the text.

Seismic Sense

Moon Ribas is a dancer and cyborg artist. She uses an online seismic sensor implanted in her feet that allows her to perceive earthquakes around the world in real time. To learn more about the device, click "seismic sensor."

Synesthesia

Synesthesia is a very rare perceptual condition in which one sense is consistently and simultaneously perceived by one or more additional senses. It is commonly said to be a, “cross-wiring” in the brain or a, “blending of the senses.” Examples of synesthesia including always perceiving a certain letter in a specific color or sensing taste when looking at different shapes. Click, "perceptual condition" to learn more.
