Vibratory Vest

Dr. Eagleman's lab is leveraging the power of sensory substition, "a non-invasive technique for circumventing the loss of one sense by feeding its information through another channel." The vest allows individuals with deafness or other hearing impairments to perceive auditory information through small vibrations on their torso. Learn more about sensory substitution and the project by clicking, "Dr. Eagleman's lab."

Hearing Color

Artist and cyborg rights activist, Neil Harbisson uses an "eyeborg" to hear the sounds of visible, infrared, and ultraviolet light. Neil was born with Achromatopsia (total color blindess) and uses an antenna to percieve electromagnetic radiation that is transmitted as audible vibrations in his skull. He is known as, "the world's first cyborg," and advocates for cyborg rights. To learn more about Neil and his sensory abilities, click "eyeborg."

Seismic Sense

Moon Ribas is a dancer and cyborg artist. She uses an online seismic sensor implanted in feet that allows her to perceive earthquakes around the world in real time. To learn more about the device, click "seismic sensor."
